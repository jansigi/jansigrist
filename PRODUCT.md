# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Anyone who lands on Jan Sigrist's personal site — recruiters and potential employers sizing up his work, fellow developers and musicians, and curious visitors who found him through GitHub, LinkedIn, or a Google search. They arrive with no fixed agenda and little patience: the site has one shot to communicate who Jan is, what he builds, and that he's worth reaching out to. The job to be done is a fast, memorable first impression that makes the next click (a project, a video, an email) feel obvious.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Jan Sigrist — a final-year software apprentice at Ergon Informatik in Zürich and a multi-instrument musician (guitar, piano, bass, drums). It exists to present two sides of one person in a single coherent place: the engineer (full-stack web, Android tooling, Kotlin DSLs, side-project games) and the musician (YouTube/Spotify playing). Success looks like a visitor coming away with a clear sense of Jan's range and personality, and an easy path to his projects, his music, or his inbox.

## Brand Personality

Sharp and editorial-restrained, with the craft doing the talking. The voice is first-person, direct, and a little dry — "I'll happily stay on a hard problem until it clicks," not corporate self-promotion. It should feel like a person with a point of view, not a résumé: quietly self-assured, precise, no hard sell. Confidence comes from restraint and typographic discipline rather than visual noise. Three words: **curious, precise, composed.**

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / startup templates.** No hero-metric card walls, no gradient-button-everywhere landing-page kit, no cookie-cutter section-icon-heading-blurb grids.
- **Corporate / stiff.** Not a LinkedIn résumé in web form. No buzzword filler, no impersonal third-person bio, no formality for formality's sake.
- **Cluttered / try-hard.** Effects and motion serve the content; no pile-up of animations and gimmicks competing for attention.
- **Bland / forgettable.** No safe, default, template-indistinguishable design. If it could be anyone's site, it has failed.

## Design Principles

1. **One person, two crafts — one voice.** The engineering and music sides should feel like facets of the same character, unified by type, color, and tone rather than split into two disconnected microsites.
2. **Show the work, don't sell it.** Let real projects, real playing, and honest first-person copy do the persuading. Confidence, not marketing.
3. **Distinctive through restraint.** A point of view is the goal, but it's carried by editorial discipline — type, spacing, one committed accent — not by loud effects. Distinctive-but-quiet beats both conventional and flashy.
4. **Motion and effect earn their place.** Every animation, hover, and transition should reward attention or aid comprehension — never decorate for its own sake.
5. **Fast and effortless.** A visitor should reach any project, video, or contact link in one obvious move. Speed and clarity are part of the impression.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text must clear 4.5:1 contrast against its background in both light and dark themes (the site already ships a full dark-mode palette and respects `prefers-color-scheme`). Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for every animation with a crossfade or instant fallback. Interactive elements (lightbox, gallery, nav) need keyboard operability and visible focus states. Images carry meaningful alt text.
